Geological Framework

Four Zones.
One Legal Opportunity.

The Globe Equity Decree adjudicates the surface alluvium tied to the Gila River mainstem. Project 88 operates in the formations beneath that boundary — geology the 1935 court never thought to foreclose.

```
Zone 01
GEC Adjudicated
Gila River Subflow Alluvium
Surface and near-surface alluvial deposits hydraulically connected to the Gila River mainstem. Governed by the Globe Equity Decree (1935). All new wells in this zone are legally classified as river diversions — subject to senior priority rights and strict curtailment.
Zone 02
Transition Layer
Deep Geological Containment
The transitional buffer between GEC-governed alluvium and unregulated deep geology. This layer defines the legal boundary of Project 88's operational zone — everything above is adjudicated; everything below is open ground.
Zone 03
Operational Core
MARS Recharge Well
The Managed Aquifer Recharge and Storage well is bored to granite-vault depth, bypassing the GEC alluvium entirely. Documented beneficial use at this depth establishes prior appropriation rights under ARS Title 45 — rights the Decree was never written to block.
Zone 04
Primary Target
Granite-Vault Aquifer Formations
Deep-seated granite-vault geology beneath the Kearny Basin — geologically distinct from the GEC-adjudicated alluvial subflow. Unregulated. Rechargeable. Capable of storing substantial water volumes independent of Gila River hydrology and senior surface rights.
```

Core Mechanism

M·A·R·S

Managed Aquifer Recharge and Storage is the technical framework that transforms Project 88 from a legal theory into operational water infrastructure.

M
Managed
Controlled recharge operations through engineered wells — not passive infiltration. Managed injection allows precise volume tracking, which is the evidentiary foundation for prior appropriation claims under Arizona water law.
A
Aquifer
The granite-vault formations in the Kearny Basin are the storage vessel. Unlike surface reservoirs or alluvial systems, deep granite-vault geology offers long-term containment with minimal evaporation loss.
R
Recharge
Surplus water from rain events, effluent, or treated water is injected during high-availability periods and stored for recovery during shortage — creating a year-round buffer independent of Gila River flows.
S
Storage
Long-term underground storage builds water security across multi-year drought cycles. Storage credits accrued under ARS Title 45 are transferable — creating infrastructure with standalone asset value beyond municipal use.

Legal Architecture

The Strategy Is in
the Boundary.

Project 88 doesn't fight the Globe Equity Decree. It operates below it — in geology the 1935 court never adjudicated and never foreclosed.

```
⚖️
Globe Equity Decree (1935)
Governs surface and alluvial subflow hydraulically connected to the Gila River mainstem. Jurisdiction is defined by proximity to the river and hydrological connectivity — not by depth or geological formation type.
// Jurisdictional boundary: alluvial subflow only
📐
ARS Title 45 — Prior Appropriation
Arizona water law recognizes prior appropriation rights for groundwater beneficially used and documented. A MARS well that injects and extracts water from granite-vault formations — below the GEC boundary — establishes legally defensible priority rights through use.
// Rights established via: documented beneficial use
🏔️
The Arbitrage
All legal value is created below the GEC line. The granite-vault formations are not subflow. They are not adjudicated. Recharging them does not trigger a senior-rights violation. The Town of Kearny gains a water supply pathway the Decree was never designed to close.
// GEC line = boundary of constraint AND opportunity
🤝
Industry Partnership Path
ASARCO and Resolution Copper have existing operational footprints in the Kearny Basin. Project 88 offers both a water stability solution for their next-century operations and a mechanism to fulfill long-term community water commitments — a negotiated transfer cannot do what a repressurized aquifer can.
// Target partners: ASARCO · Resolution Copper · Water.org
```
